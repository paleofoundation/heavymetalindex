## Status

These terms are under development with the Paleo Foundation's counsel. The language below is a placeholder outlining the intended structure. Final terms will be published before the site moves to general availability. Use of the site during the pre-launch period is subject to the placeholder provisions below.

## Public research access

The Heavy Metal Index is published for public research access. Readers are welcome to consult the site, cite individual pages in academic, regulatory, journalistic, and professional work, and follow the source-provenance links to the underlying literature. Citation guidance will be published on this page prior to general availability.

## Commercial use

Commercial use of the Heavy Metal Index content, including bulk extraction, wholesale reproduction, and derivative works produced for commercial benefit, requires a license from the Paleo Foundation. The European Union's database-rights framework applies. Commercial licensing inquiries: see [contact](/contact).

## Scraping and automated access

Automated crawling for indexing purposes (including but not limited to Google, Google Scholar, and academic indexing services) is welcomed and is not restricted by these terms. Wholesale scraping for commercial redistribution is not permitted and is a breach of these terms and of applicable database-rights law.

## No warranty

The Heavy Metal Index reports what the cited literature supports. It is a reference, not advice. Users are responsible for their own regulatory compliance, their own risk assessments, and their own decisions. The Paleo Foundation makes no warranty as to fitness for any particular purpose, including but not limited to litigation, product formulation, and regulatory submission. The index is a scientific reference and should be treated as such.

## Correction reporting

Reports of errors, outdated claims, or broken provenance are welcomed. Submission does not create an obligation on the Foundation to publish a correction on any specific timeline, but every report is logged and reviewed. See [contact](/contact).
