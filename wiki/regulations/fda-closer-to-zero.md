# FDA Closer to Zero — Program Overview

_This page is a stub. Substantive content lands after the ingest of the FDA Closer to Zero Action Plan document._

Closer to Zero is the US Food and Drug Administration's program to reduce childhood dietary exposure to lead, cadmium, inorganic arsenic, and mercury by establishing action levels for foods commonly consumed by babies and young children. The program is staged: the agency first issues draft action levels for comment, then finalizes guidance, then uses those levels in surveillance and enforcement.

This overview page will summarize the program's structure, milestones, and current status. Matrix-specific and metal-specific action levels that the program has finalized or proposed will live on their own regulation pages and link back here.
