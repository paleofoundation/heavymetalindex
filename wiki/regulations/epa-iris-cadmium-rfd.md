# EPA IRIS — Cadmium Oral Reference Dose

_This page is a stub. The operative EPA IRIS oral RfD for cadmium is the 1985 assessment on IRIS chemical record 0141; the raw document for that operative record has not been placed in this wiki's corpus and is flagged as a pending ingest. A 1999 EPA external review draft exists in `raw/reports/` (`EPA_IRIS_Cadmium_ToxicologicalReview.pdf`) but is marked "EXTERNAL REVIEW DRAFT - DO NOT QUOTE," never became Agency position, and has not been ingested; it is retained for a future historical ingest wave because it documents EPA's reassessment attempt. See the 2026-04-24 log entry `epa-iris-deferred` for the rationale._

The Integrated Risk Information System is the US Environmental Protection Agency's database of chronic human health effect values. The oral reference dose for cadmium is EPA's estimate of a daily oral exposure to the human population that is likely to be without appreciable risk of deleterious effects during a lifetime. It is a toxicity value, not an enforceable standard, but US regulatory and advisory decisions in multiple domains (drinking water, soil cleanup, food) reference it.

## RfD value

_Pending ingest of the operative 1985 IRIS chemical record 0141. The operative values will be recorded here exactly as they appear in the IRIS assessment, with uncertainty factors and critical study captured. Values summarized in secondary sources as the operative IRIS RfDs are approximately 5 × 10⁻⁴ mg/kg/day for cadmium in water and 1 × 10⁻³ mg/kg/day for cadmium in food, anchored to a kidney-cortex concentration of 200 µg/g as the critical effect; these figures cannot be recorded here as verified until the raw IRIS record is in the corpus and a source page with SHA-256 provenance is created._

## How this compares to EFSA TWI, JECFA PTMI, and ATSDR MRLs

_Pending. This comparison is important because the values rest on overlapping primary literature but apply different agency methodologies and policy frames. EPA IRIS operates against a daily-intake RfD with uncertainty factors; EFSA CONTAM uses a weekly-intake TWI derived via chemical-specific adjustment factors from a biomarker-based BMDL5; JECFA uses a monthly-intake PTMI reflecting cadmium's long half-life; ATSDR MRLs are route- and duration-specific minimal risk levels for public-health assessment. Direct numeric comparison requires translating across averaging windows and across the different endpoint anchors._

## Historical reassessment draft (1999)

EPA produced an external review draft "Toxicological Review: Cadmium and Compounds (CAS No. 7440-43-9)" dated March 4, 1999, circulated for internal Agency peer review. The document was explicitly marked "EXTERNAL REVIEW DRAFT - DO NOT QUOTE" and "should not at this stage be construed to represent Agency position on this chemical." It proposed a reassessment of the 1985 IRIS RfD and RfC values. The draft appears never to have been finalized; the operative IRIS record for cadmium remains the 1985 assessment. The 1999 draft is worth separate ingest as a historical document, because it documents EPA's reassessment attempt and the reasoning it contained is citable in regulatory or litigation contexts (regulators or plaintiff's experts may reference it as evidence that EPA reviewed the assessment and elected not to adopt a revision). A later ingest wave will create its own source page with explicit "DRAFT, NOT ADOPTED" flagging.
