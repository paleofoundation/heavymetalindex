# EPA IRIS — Cadmium Oral Reference Doses

The EPA Integrated Risk Information System chemical assessment for cadmium provides two route-specific oral reference doses that reflect the different absorption efficiency of cadmium in water versus food matrices ([[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]]). Both values were last revised October 1, 1989 and remain in force ([[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]]).

| Parameter | Water RfD | Food RfD |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Oral RfD | **5 × 10⁻⁴ mg/kg/day** | **1 × 10⁻³ mg/kg/day** |
| Daily equivalent (µg/kg/day) | 0.5 | 1.0 |
| NOAEL | 5 × 10⁻³ mg/kg/day | 1 × 10⁻² mg/kg/day |
| Composite uncertainty factor | 10 | 10 |
| Critical effect | Significant proteinuria | Significant proteinuria |
| EPA confidence | High | High |
| Last revised | 1989-10-01 | 1989-10-01 |

## Critical effect and derivation

The critical endpoint is significant proteinuria, indicating renal tubular dysfunction, the same canonical cadmium endpoint that anchors the [[sources/efsa-cadmium-contam-2009|EFSA 2009]] tolerable weekly intake and the [[sources/atsdr-cadmium-toxprofile-2012|ATSDR 2012]] chronic oral Minimal Risk Level. The route-specific RfD distinction is a methodological feature unique to the EPA approach: cadmium absorption from drinking water is approximately twofold higher than from food matrices, so equivalent body burden protection requires a tighter water RfD than food RfD ([[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]]).

The composite uncertainty factor of 10 reflects EPA's standard approach for human variability when the critical effect is observed in human chronic-exposure cohort studies (occupational and environmentally exposed populations), reducing the need for animal-to-human extrapolation factors ([[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]]). The "high confidence" classification reflects both the strength of the underlying epidemiological evidence and the consistency across studies ([[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]]).

## Status of the 1999 reassessment draft

A 1999 EPA external review draft for cadmium (EPA/635/R-99/000-something), retained in `raw/reports/EPA_IRIS_Cadmium_ToxicologicalReview.pdf`, proposed a reassessment that was never finalized. The 1989 RfD values therefore remain the operative US federal reference ([[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]]). The 1999 draft is documented separately in the [[synthesis]] page as a historical document worth ingesting in a future wave because it demonstrates EPA reviewed and chose not to adopt a revision, which is part of the defensibility record.

## Comparison to other reference values

| Body / Jurisdiction | Value (original units) | Daily equivalent (µg/kg b.w./day) | Ratio to ATSDR chronic oral MRL | Source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ATSDR chronic oral MRL (US, 2012) | 0.1 µg/kg/day | 0.1 | 1 | [[sources/atsdr-cadmium-toxprofile-2012]] |
| EFSA TWI (EU, 2009) | 2.5 µg/kg b.w./week | ≈ 0.36 | 3.6x | [[sources/efsa-cadmium-contam-2009]] |
| JECFA PTMI (international, 2010) | 25 µg/kg b.w./month | ≈ 0.83 | 8.3x | [[sources/jecfa-73rd-cadmium-2010]] |
| EPA IRIS oral RfD, food (US, 1989) | 1 × 10⁻³ mg/kg/day | 1.0 | 10x | [[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]] |
| EPA IRIS oral RfD, water (US, 1989) | 5 × 10⁻⁴ mg/kg/day | 0.5 | 5x | [[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]] |

The EPA IRIS food RfD is the most permissive of the four major dietary cadmium reference values when expressed on a daily per-body-weight basis ([[sources/epa-iris-cadmium-1989]]). The 1989 vintage of the assessment, contrasted with the more recent ATSDR (2012) and EFSA (2009) values, partly explains the divergence.
