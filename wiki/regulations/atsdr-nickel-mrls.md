# ATSDR — Minimal Risk Levels for Nickel

The US Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry has derived Minimal Risk Levels (MRLs) for nickel covering oral and inhalation routes across acute, intermediate, and chronic durations in its October 2024 Toxicological Profile for Nickel ([[sources/atsdr-nickel-toxprofile-2024|ATSDR Ni 2024]]). ATSDR MRLs are used in US public health assessments at hazardous waste sites under CERCLA Section 104(i) and in community exposure investigations; they are not enforceable standards but are routinely referenced by state agencies and in litigation. The MRL framework parallels ATSDR's published profiles for [[regulations/atsdr-cadmium-mrls|cadmium]] and [[regulations/atsdr-aluminum-mrls|aluminum]], using the same route-and-duration matrix and the same overall derivation methodology (point-of-departure selection, uncertainty-factor application, and benchmark-dose modeling where data permit).

The 2024 ATSDR profile is the most recent comprehensive US toxicology synthesis for nickel and is one of the four secondary references on which the [[metals/nickel|nickel metal page]] currently rests. Specific MRL values by route and duration require transcription from the source PDF, which is staged at `raw/reports/Bookshelf_NBK610351.pdf` per the source-page provenance ([[sources/atsdr-nickel-toxprofile-2024|ATSDR Ni 2024]]).

## Derivation parameters

The point-of-departure studies, critical endpoints, benchmark-dose models, and applied uncertainty factors for each MRL above will be populated from the [[sources/atsdr-nickel-toxprofile-2024|ATSDR Ni 2024]] profile during the primary-source ingest pass. The structure mirrors the [[regulations/atsdr-cadmium-mrls|ATSDR cadmium MRL page]]: a per-MRL derivation table covering the critical study, the critical endpoint, the LOAEL or NOAEL, any benchmark-dose calculation, the duration adjustment for inhalation routes, and the uncertainty factors applied (typically 10 each for animal-to-human extrapolation, human variability, and LOAEL-to-NOAEL extrapolation where applicable).
