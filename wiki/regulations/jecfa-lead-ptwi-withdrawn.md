# JECFA — Lead Provisional Tolerable Weekly Intake (Withdrawn 2010)

The Joint FAO/WHO Expert Committee on Food Additives, at its 72nd meeting in June 2010, withdrew the long-standing provisional tolerable weekly intake of 25 µg lead per kilogram body weight per week and replaced it with no quantitative international reference value ([[sources/jecfa-72nd-lead-2010|JECFA 72nd 2010]]). The Committee concluded that the dose-response data for the critical endpoints (developmental neurotoxicity in children, cardiovascular and renal effects in adults) do not support a threshold-based PTWI and that no value could be considered health-protective at observed general-population dietary exposures ([[sources/jecfa-72nd-lead-2010|JECFA 72nd 2010]]).

This withdrawal is the source of the "no safe level of lead" framing that propagates through all subsequent regulatory documents (EFSA 2010 ([[sources/efsa-lead-contam-2010|EFSA Pb 2010]]), ATSDR 2020 ([[sources/atsdr-lead-toxprofile-2020|ATSDR 2020]]), FDA Closer to Zero 2025 ([[sources/fda-ctz-Pb-babyfood-2025|FDA CTZ Pb 2025]]), CDC BLRV 2021 ([[sources/cdc-blood-lead-reference-value|CDC BLRV]])). Codex Alimentarius has not adopted a replacement health-based reference value for lead; matrix-specific maximum levels in CXS 193-1995 continue to operate but are not aligned to a single dietary reference ([[sources/codex-cxs-193-1995|Codex CXS 193-1995]]).
