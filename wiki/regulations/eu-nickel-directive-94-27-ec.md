# EU — Nickel Restriction on Skin-Contact Articles (Directive 94/27/EC, now REACH Annex XVII)

The European Union restricts the migration of nickel from articles intended to come into direct and prolonged contact with human skin to a release rate of less than 0.5 µg Ni per cm² per week. The restriction was first introduced as Council Directive 94/27/EC of 30 June 1994 (amending Directive 76/769/EEC on the restriction of marketing and use of certain dangerous substances) and has since been incorporated into the consolidated REACH framework as Annex XVII, Entry 27 of Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006. The 0.5 µg Ni/cm²/week release-rate limit is the operative regulatory parameter for jewelry, watches, eyeglass frames, clothing fasteners, and similar items in long-term contact with skin in the EU market.

The restriction's policy rationale is the prevention and mitigation of nickel allergic contact dermatitis at the population level. Sustained skin contact with nickel-releasing items is the dominant gateway exposure for nickel sensitization in European populations, and the gateway-exposure intervention is the lever the restriction was designed to pull. The restriction does not address dietary nickel exposure, which is governed by the [[regulations/efsa-nickel-tdi|EFSA chronic TDI]] and the EFSA acute LOAEL framework ([[sources/efsa-nickel-contam-2020|EFSA 2020]]).

## Scope distinctions within the restriction

The single 0.5 µg/cm²/week parameter on this page is the headline limit referenced in the [[metals/nickel|nickel metal page]] regulatory-limits table. The full Annex XVII Entry 27 framework contains additional sub-restrictions (a tighter parameter for body-piercing post assemblies, a release-rate-after-coating-wear requirement, and exemptions for specific article categories) that are not transcribed here pending ingest of the primary REACH Annex XVII regulatory text. The Annex XVII consolidated text is the appropriate primary source; a future ingest should populate this page from that document and any current European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) guidance.

## Relationship to other nickel reference values

The EU skin-contact release rate is the only major nickel regulation expressed in cm²/week migration units rather than mg/kg or µg/kg-bw/day; it is not directly comparable to the dietary references. It targets a different exposure route (skin contact, leading to sensitization) than the dietary-intake references like the [[regulations/efsa-nickel-tdi|EFSA chronic TDI]] and the EFSA acute LOAEL ([[sources/efsa-nickel-contam-2020|EFSA 2020]]), but the two regulatory regimes are mechanistically connected: the skin-contact restriction is the upstream intervention that determines what fraction of the European population becomes nickel-sensitized and therefore subject to the EFSA acute LOAEL of 4.3 µg Ni/kg b.w. for systemic contact dermatitis flare-ups in sensitized individuals ([[sources/efsa-nickel-contam-2020|EFSA 2020]]).
