# California Proposition 65 — Lead and Lead Compounds Listing

Lead and lead compounds are listed under California's Proposition 65 in two separate categories: as chemicals known to the state to cause cancer, and as chemicals known to the state to cause reproductive toxicity (developmental, male, and female endpoints) ([[sources/oehha-lead-prop65-listing]]). Each listing carries its own quantitative implementation threshold for warning-label requirements: a No Significant Risk Level for the cancer listing, and Maximum Allowable Daily Levels for the reproductive-toxicity listings (oral and inhalation routes). Consumer products sold in California that expose a consumer above the relevant threshold must carry a Prop 65 warning ([[sources/oehha-lead-prop65-listing]]).

This page records the listing structure and flags the quantitative implementation documents (NSRL and MADL) as pending separate ingest. The OEHHA Prop 65 cadmium listing structure is parallel ([[regulations/oehha-cadmium-prop65]] for the reproductive listing; the cadmium NSRL is also pending separate ingest).
