# FDA Closer to Zero — Lead Action Level for Single-Ingredient Root Vegetables (Baby Food)

_This page is a stub. Substantive content lands after the ingest of the FDA guidance document that sets this action level._

Under the Closer to Zero program, FDA has established a separate lead action level for processed single-ingredient root vegetables intended for babies and young children less than two years old. Root vegetables receive their own threshold because carrots and sweet potatoes accumulate lead from soil at rates substantially higher than other vegetable crops, and because they are a nutritionally important commodity for this age group for which a tighter threshold would affect market availability.
