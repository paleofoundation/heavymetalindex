_This page is a stub. Substantive content lands after paired ingest of the 1996 OEHHA evidence document that supported the carcinogenicity listing and the later OEHHA document establishing the Maximum Allowable Dose Level._

Cadmium is listed under California's Proposition 65 as a chemical known to the state to cause cancer, and subsequently the Office of Environmental Health Hazard Assessment established a Maximum Allowable Dose Level for cadmium under the same statute. The MADL is the quantitative implementation; the 1996 evidence record is its scientific provenance.
