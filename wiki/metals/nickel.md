Nickel (Ni, atomic number 28) is a transition metal classified by the International Agency for Research on Cancer as Group 1 (carcinogenic to humans) for nickel compounds and Group 2B (possibly carcinogenic) for metallic nickel. For non-occupationally exposed general populations, human exposure is overwhelmingly dietary rather than by inhalation, with cocoa, whole grains, legumes, nuts, shellfish, and leafy vegetables carrying the highest concentrations in typical diets. The wiki treats inhalation and dietary exposure as distinct toxicological regimes with distinct evidence bases, and maintains nickel speciation (ionic versus particulate, and compound-specific behavior for NiS, Ni3S2, NiO, and Ni(CO)4) where the source supports the distinction.
