Tin (Sn, atomic number 50) in the food system arises principally from tinplate-lined canned products, where acidic contents can mobilize tin from the lining; from some food contact materials; and from certain organotin stabilizers historically used in food-contact plastics. Inorganic tin is less acutely toxic than most heavy metals in scope of this wiki, but high-acid canned products can produce meaningful exposures and are the subject of specific regulatory limits in several jurisdictions. Organotin compounds, historically used as biocides and plastic stabilizers, present a distinct and higher-concern toxicological profile.
