Aluminum (Al, atomic number 13) is the most abundant metal in the earth's crust and is ubiquitous in the food system. Dietary aluminum derives from natural levels in plant foods, from cookware and food-contact materials, from water treatment, and from direct addition as food additives (notably aluminum-containing leavening agents, certain colorants, and some anticaking agents). The toxicological picture is dominated by chronic exposure with a particular focus on renal-impaired populations and on infants via early-life formula and ingredient exposures.
