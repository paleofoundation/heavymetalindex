## Overview

Lead is a non-essential heavy metal with documented neurodevelopmental toxicity at exposures that occur in segments of the general population, particularly children. No safe level of lead exposure has been identified for children's health. The metal enters the human food system predominantly through atmospheric deposition onto crops, uptake from contaminated soil, and contact with legacy lead-containing equipment in food processing. Children absorb a substantially higher fraction of ingested lead than adults, and their developing nervous systems are most susceptible to its effects.

## Reference values

FDA Interim Reference Levels for dietary lead, updated 2022:

| Population | IRL (µg/day) |
| --- | --- |
| Children | 2.2 |
| Women of childbearing age | 8.8 |

Derived from the CDC blood lead reference value of 3.5 µg/dL and dietary-to-blood-lead conversion factors with an additional 10x safety factor. JECFA withdrew its provisional tolerable weekly intake for lead in 2011, concluding no PTWI could be considered health protective.
