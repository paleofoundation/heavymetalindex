Lead (Pb, atomic number 82) is a neurotoxic heavy metal with well-characterized dose-response relationships for adverse outcomes in children and adults. Lead exposure in food originates from soil uptake in cultivation, from lead-containing equipment in food processing, from lead solder in older canned-food production, and from environmental deposition. Regulatory authorities in the United States, the European Union, and other jurisdictions have increasingly concluded that no safe threshold of lead exposure in food can be identified for sensitive populations.
