Core toxic-metal pages describe toxicology, typical exposure routes through food, food sources linked to [ingredients](/ingredients), regulatory limits linked to [regulations](/regulations), analytical methods linked to [testing](/testing), and microbiome effects where documented. Arsenic and mercury are maintained under separate pages for the inorganic and total analytical quantities, because the toxicological and regulatory implications of the two species differ sharply. Nutrient elements such as [[metals/manganese]] and [[metals/zinc]] are included as graph stubs when they appear in infant-formula risk-assessment sources, but they are not HMTc toxic-metal analytes unless separately designated.
