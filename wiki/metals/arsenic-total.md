Total arsenic (tAs) denotes the sum of inorganic and organic arsenic species in a sample. Organic arsenic species, including arsenobetaine (the principal form in most seafood) and arsenosugars (principal form in seaweed), are substantially less toxic to humans than the inorganic forms. Total arsenic is a meaningful analytical quantity, particularly in contexts where speciation is not performed, but it does not by itself support a health-based interpretation; for that purpose see [arsenic, inorganic](/metals/arsenic-inorganic).

[[sources/ufelle2021-metals-chapter|Ufelle & Barchowsky 2021]] is attached to this analytical-quantity page only as arsenic-species context. The chapter reinforces why the wiki cannot treat total arsenic as a health-equivalent substitute for inorganic arsenic.

## WHO GEMS/Food Occurrence Context

GEMS/Food contributes a broad total-arsenic occurrence map across water, seafood, algae/seaweed, rice, vegetable, supplement, and infant-food rows. Total arsenic is retained separately from inorganic arsenic because marine and algae rows can be dominated by organic arsenicals, while rice and some infant-cereal rows are more relevant to iAs review.
