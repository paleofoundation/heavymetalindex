## Overview

Cadmium is a non-essential heavy metal with documented renal, skeletal, and carcinogenic toxicity at exposures that occur in segments of the general population. It enters the human food system predominantly through plant uptake from contaminated soils and atmospheric deposition, with the highest concentrations observed in organ meats, shellfish, cereal grains, leafy vegetables, cocoa, and tobacco. Body burden accumulates over decades because the biological half-life in the kidney is measured in years rather than days.
