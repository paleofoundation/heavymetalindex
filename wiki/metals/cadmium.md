Cadmium (Cd, atomic number 48) is a toxic heavy metal that accumulates in the kidney cortex over decades of exposure and is associated with renal dysfunction and reduced bone mineral density at chronic dietary exposures within range of ordinary population intakes in some geographies. Cadmium in food originates primarily from soil uptake, with cocoa, leafy green vegetables, shellfish, and organ meats among the higher-concentration food matrices. Cultivar selection, soil phosphate and zinc status, and regional geochemistry are the principal drivers of variance.
