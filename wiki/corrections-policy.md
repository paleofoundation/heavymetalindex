# Corrections Policy

Heavy Metal Index treats corrections as part of the product, not as an embarrassment to hide. A public evidence map is only as trustworthy as its willingness to show what changed, why it changed, and how the correction was made.

## What can be reported

Correction submissions may address:

- wrong or stale regulatory values;
- incorrect numeric extraction;
- incorrect source attribution;
- broken provenance links;
- wrong analyte species, basis, unit, or product routing;
- material omissions where a public page clearly missed a source-backed qualification or contradiction.

## How to submit a correction

Use the editorial contact route described on [contact](/contact). A useful submission includes:

- the page URL or cite key;
- the exact sentence, table cell, or claim at issue;
- the proposed correction;
- the supporting source or document reference;
- the submitter's name, role, and reason for contact, if they want a response.

Anonymous tips may still be reviewed, but named submissions with source citations are easier to verify quickly.

## Triage

Corrections are triaged by severity.

### Critical

Claims that could materially mislead a regulator, journalist, litigator, retailer, or standards user:

- wrong threshold;
- wrong number;
- wrong source;
- wrong species or basis;
- false statement of what a source supports.

Critical issues are prioritized ahead of routine page maintenance.

### Major

Claims that are directionally relevant but materially incomplete or misleading:

- missing caveat;
- wrong routing;
- stale interpretation after source-family update;
- public synthesis that overstates certainty.

### Minor

Formatting, labeling, summary, or link issues that do not materially change the scientific reading.

## Correction workflow

The correction workflow is:

1. intake and scope the report;
2. verify against the cited source or page history;
3. correct the affected public page or attach a visible qualification if the issue is still under review;
4. record the event in the public [log](/log) when the correction is material;
5. extend the correction to related pages or generated files if the same error class may recur elsewhere.

## No silent substantive corrections

Substantive corrections are not made invisibly. If a numeric or interpretive public claim changed in a way that matters to a serious reader, the change belongs in version history and, when appropriate, in the public log or page-level note.

## Relationship to disputes

Not every disagreement is a correction. If the issue is a substantive interpretation dispute rather than a clear factual error, it moves into the [dispute resolution policy](/dispute-resolution-policy).
