# Conflict of Interest Policy

The Heavy Metal Index is operated by the same foundation that operates the Heavy Metal Tested & Certified program. That shared operator is the central conflict-of-interest question for the site, so it is disclosed openly rather than hidden.

## Baseline disclosure

The operator relationship is permanent and structural:

- Heavy Metal Index is operated by the Paleo Foundation.
- Heavy Metal Tested & Certified is also operated by the Paleo Foundation.
- The Index is the public evidence layer.
- HMT&C is the downstream certification program.

The conflict policy exists to keep those roles from collapsing into one another.

## Conflict categories

Potential conflicts include:

- financial relationships tied to certification, consulting, or licensing work;
- operational involvement in HMT&C threshold setting;
- participation in client-specific advisory or litigation work related to a claim under review;
- authorship, funding, or close affiliation with a source being summarized;
- direct reputational or commercial benefit from a particular public interpretation of the evidence.

## Editorial rule

No public Heavy Metal Index claim may be strengthened, softened, or framed to make HMT&C look more justified than the literature supports. Likewise, HMT&C thresholds may not be used as evidence for a public Index claim.

## Recusal triggers

Recusal is required when a person would otherwise be the sole public approver of:

- a page or claim that bears directly on an active HMT&C threshold question;
- a correction or dispute involving a source, client, or certification matter in which that person has material involvement;
- a page where the reviewer is also the author, funder, or direct advocate for the source being interpreted.

In those cases, the conflicted person may still provide technical context, but the final public-use decision must not rest with that person alone.

## Handling procedure

When a conflict is material to a public page:

- the conflict is identified internally at review time;
- the page or claim is routed to a second reviewer or governance check;
- the public page continues to present the literature basis rather than the certification basis;
- if the conflict changes how a reader should interpret the page, a visible disclosure note is added.

## Brand and client boundary

The public Index does not publish brand-specific internal testing, client COAs, or client-confidential evidence packages. Those materials belong in private systems, not in the public evidence map.

## Why this policy is public

For a site like this, the strongest conflict policy is not pretending there is no tension. It is making the tension visible, constraining it, and publishing the rules that keep the evidence library from turning into a disguised certification brochure.
