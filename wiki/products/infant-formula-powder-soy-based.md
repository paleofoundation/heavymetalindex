## Data Notes

- Heavy-metal values from Akhtar et al. 2017 are stored as **mg/kg** for Fe, Zn, Ni, Pb, and Cd because Table 4 and the discussion use mg/kg. The abstract's ug/kg wording for Fe/Zn/Ni is treated as a source-text unit error.
- Ni maximum: 50.90 mg/kg as-sold powder, equivalent to 50,900 ug/kg.
- Pb and Cd were reported below detection in every brand, but the numeric Pb/Cd non-detect thresholds differ between Methods and Table 4. The public page therefore reports the non-detect finding and keeps the exact LOD under unit review.
- AFM1 is not part of the Heavy Metal Index metal panel, but it is retained here as co-contaminant context from the same infant formula survey.

## Basis and QA Comparator Notes

Formula QA COAs are usually dry-powder-as-sold. Prepared-for-feeding values and dry-powder values are not directly comparable without dilution and unit normalization. QA teams should compare only like with like: same analyte/species, same basis, same unit, compatible sample preparation, and documented LOQ/LOD or censoring treatment. A COA on a dry ingredient, a finished product as sold, a prepared serving, and a pooled market basket are separate evidence objects.
