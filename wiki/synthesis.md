This page carries the current best understanding of the heavy-metals-in-food literature in compressed form. It is revised as the corpus is ingested and as new sources are added.

## Status

Synthesis content is not yet published. The wiki is in pre-launch; ingest of the corpus is underway. This page exists as a stub so the scaffolding is reviewable and future updates have a stable location. Substantive synthesis material will appear here after the corpus has been processed to a coverage threshold sufficient to support top-line claims without bias toward the papers that happened to be ingested first.

## Planned structure

When populated, this page will be organized around the eight in-scope metals with a short synthesis for each covering: the metals of greatest dietary concern by population and life stage, the food categories that dominate population-level exposure, the trend in regulatory posture across major jurisdictions, and the areas of active scientific disagreement. Claims on this page will be terse and will link to the canonical metal, ingredient, product-category, regulation, and source pages for detail.

The synthesis page is intended as the entry point for readers who want the highest-level view of what the literature supports. It is not a substitute for reading the primary literature; it is a summary whose claims are auditable by following the links.
