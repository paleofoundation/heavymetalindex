# Current synthesis

This page is the evolving statement of the Heavy Metal Index's current best understanding across the metals in scope. It is updated when ingests move the needle, and it is always behind the primary-source pages it draws from.

At present the synthesis is empty because the first ingest batch has not yet completed. The first substantive synthesis entry will cover cadmium in food, combining the Handbook on the Toxicology of Metals chapter (Nordberg, Nogawa, Nordberg, 2015) as the canonical toxicological frame with the four regulatory risk assessments that derive dose-response guidance from that base: the EFSA CONTAM Panel scientific opinion, the EPA IRIS toxicological review, the JECFA monograph, and the ATSDR toxicological profile. California's Proposition 65 listing and the current Codex Alimentarius maximum levels will contextualize the US-state and international regulatory postures. A first-draft cadmium synthesis will be posted when those ingests are complete.

Readers should treat any claim here as provisional and trace it upward to the source page before citing.
