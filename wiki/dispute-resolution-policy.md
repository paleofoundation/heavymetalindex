# Dispute Resolution Policy

The Heavy Metal Index distinguishes between a correction request and a substantive dispute.

A correction request says: this page is factually wrong. A dispute says: this source base may support more than one reasonable interpretation, or the page may have drawn the wrong conclusion from contested evidence.

## What qualifies as a dispute

Disputes may involve:

- disagreement over how a source should be interpreted;
- disagreement over whether a source is product-fit for a specific page;
- disagreement over inclusion, exclusion, or weighting of evidence in a synthesis;
- disagreement over whether a public caveat is strong enough;
- disagreement over whether a claim class should remain public while evidence is contested.

## Intake

Disputes are submitted through the same public contact route used for corrections, but should identify themselves as disputes and include:

- the page URL or claim being challenged;
- the reason the interpretation is contested;
- the source material or counter-source being relied on;
- whether the dispute is about fact, fit, framing, or omission.

## Review path

Disputes are reviewed in four steps.

1. Determine whether the issue is actually a factual correction.
2. If it is interpretive, assemble the directly relevant source set.
3. Re-check the current page against that source set and the editorial firewall.
4. Decide whether the page should be corrected, clarified, temporarily flagged, or left unchanged with rationale.

## Temporary visible flags

When a dispute affects a material public claim on a high-value page, the page may carry a visible note while review is ongoing. The purpose of the note is not to dramatize uncertainty; it is to avoid the false impression that a contested claim is settled when it is still under active review.

## Outcomes

A dispute may resolve in one of five ways:

- correction of a factual or interpretive error;
- clarification or added caveat with no change to the underlying number;
- no change, with internal record of why the current interpretation was retained;
- split-view presentation where the literature genuinely supports two distinct readings and the page must say so explicitly;
- temporary withdrawal of a claim from public use pending better evidence or a full resynthesis.

## Firewall rule

If the dispute is really about an HMT&C threshold or certification decision rather than about what the literature supports, it does not get settled by changing the public Index to fit the certification outcome. The Index remains the literature baseline. Certification rationale belongs on the certification side.

## Documentation

Material dispute outcomes are documented through the public log, affected page history, or both, depending on severity and scope.
