Testing pages describe analytical methods for heavy metals in food, including inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry, atomic absorption spectrometry, X-ray fluorescence, and speciation protocols for arsenic and mercury. Each method page covers the analytical principle, what the method does and does not measure, typical limits of detection and quantification for each metal, sample-preparation pitfalls, and cost and throughput considerations. Sampling-plan design is addressed on a dedicated page because a limit is only as good as the sampling protocol that produced the datum.
