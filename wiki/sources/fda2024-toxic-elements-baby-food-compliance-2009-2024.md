# FDA 2024 — Toxic Elements Compliance Results For Baby And Young Child Foods

## TL;DR

This FDA table reports compliance-program analytical results for arsenic, lead, cadmium, and mercury in foods intended for babies and young children from FY2009 through FY2024. It is a high-priority ingest source because it provides row-level regulatory surveillance data across many Category 1 product types.

## Key numbers

- Arsenic table: 575 samples from FY2009-FY2024.
- Lead table: 598 samples from FY2009-FY2024.
- Cadmium table: 576 samples from FY2009-FY2024.
- Mercury table: 195 samples from FY2009-FY2024.
- Reported arsenic LOQs ranged from 0.7 to 103 ppb and LODs ranged from 0.2 to 34.3 ppb.
- Reported lead LOQs ranged from 0.2 to 100 ppb and LODs ranged from 0.1 to 33.3 ppb.
- Reported cadmium LOQs ranged from 0.1 to 63 ppb.
- Reported mercury LOQs ranged from 0.8 to 38.4 ppb and LODs ranged from 0.1 to 12.8 ppb for most mercury analyses.

## Methods (brief)

The table is an FDA compliance-program analytical dataset. Most arsenic, lead, cadmium, and mercury analyses were conducted by ICP-MS, with the PDF preserving results, LOD/LOQ handling notes, and sample metadata.

## Limitations

This is a dataset source, not a narrative paper. The PDF should be converted into structured concentration rows before product-page p50/p90/p100 aggregation. The source page records the dataset and high-level scope only.

## Implications

Certification: Priority A-tier dataset for aggregate distribution building across Category 1 product rows.

Courses: Useful example of surveillance data, non-detect handling, and why LOQ/LOD metadata must travel with results.

App: Supports retrieval and ranking for nearly all infant and child food categories.

Microbiome: No direct microbiome endpoint.
