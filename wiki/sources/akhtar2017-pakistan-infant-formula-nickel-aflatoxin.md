# Akhtar et al. 2017 - Pakistani Infant Formula AFM1 and Metals

## TL;DR

Akhtar et al. measured aflatoxin M1 and selected metals in 13 commercially available powdered infant formula milk brands sold in Pakistani markets for infants aged 1 to 6 months. For Heavy Metal Index purposes, the usable metal findings are limited context: Pb and Cd were reported below detection in every brand, while Ni ranged from below detection to 50.90 mg/kg on an as-sold powder basis. The paper does not report whether any formula was soy-based, so it should not be used as row-specific evidence for the split non-soy and soy powder formula pages.

## Evidence Fitness

For generic powdered infant formula, this source is EF-3 / Modeled or limited evidence. It provides brand-level means, ranges, non-detect findings, and source-level exceedance percentages rather than sample-level data or source-reported percentiles.

For the Category 1 split rows [[products/infant-formula-powder-non-soy]] and [[products/infant-formula-powder-soy-based]], this source is EF-4 / Context only. The paper reports infant formula milk brands but does not disclose whether the products were milk-based, soy-based, or mixed; it also anonymizes brands. The source can support a public statement that a Pakistani powdered-formula survey found Pb/Cd non-detects and high Ni in some brands, but it cannot support a row-specific concentration estimate for either split formula row.

## Methods Brief

Powdered formula was prepared for AFM1 by mixing 1 g powder with 10 mL water, defatting, and running ELISA. Heavy metals were measured from 0.5 g powder after nitric/perchloric acid digestion using flame atomic absorption spectrophotometry. The source reports LODs for Fe, Zn, Ni, Pb, and Cd in mg/kg. The Pb/Cd non-detect threshold differs between the Methods section and Table 4 by a factor of ten; HMI therefore stores the qualitative non-detect finding and flags the exact LOD for unit review.

## Implications

- Certification: do not use this source to set or justify soy-specific or non-soy-specific formula thresholds. It is useful as a surveillance signal that Ni can be high in some powdered formula brands and that Pb/Cd reporting must preserve LOD caveats.
- Courses: good teaching example for unit governance. The abstract says ug/kg for Fe/Zn/Ni, but the table and discussion say mg/kg; the table/discussion unit controls.
- App: may be used as a context source for Pakistan-market powdered formula, not as a global default contamination profile.
- Microbiome: no direct metal-microbiome content.
