# Analytical Workbench

Heavy Metal Index has two distinct assistant contexts.

The public site offers **Ask the Index**, a citation-backed evidence assistant that answers from published Heavy Metal Index pages only. It is designed for public-reference questions about sources, regulations, product categories, evidence gaps, and documented calculation inputs.

The **Analytical Workbench** is the separate private layer for confidential analytical workflows. It is where brand, retailer, auditor, and standards users can work with non-public data such as certificates of analysis, lot-level results, supplier metadata, formulation details, internal specifications, testing records, and candidate calculation scenarios.

## Why the split exists

The public assistant and the private workbench solve related but different problems.

- The public assistant explains what the published evidence says.
- The private workbench applies methods to confidential operational data.

That split protects privacy, preserves the editorial firewall, and keeps the public reference assistant from quietly turning into a confidential decision engine.

## Public Ask the Index

Public Ask the Index is designed for:

- explaining product, ingredient, metal, and regulation pages;
- comparing published evidence and directly comparable public reference values;
- showing what evidence is loaded and what is still missing;
- describing calculation inputs, limitations, and comparability rules from the public site.

It is not the right place to submit confidential, private, regulated, or sensitive information.

## Private Analytical Workbench

The private workbench is designed for workflows such as:

- lot-level percentile placement;
- supplier benchmarking;
- ingredient-supplier evidence review;
- confidential COA and lab-result review;
- release, hold, and escalation support;
- category-specific candidate standards math;
- achievability and sensitivity analysis on non-public datasets;
- internal audit and corrective-action support.

## Data types that belong in the private workbench

Examples include:

- certificates of analysis;
- supplier scorecards;
- lot identifiers and batch histories;
- SKU-specific formulation details;
- internal sampling plans;
- retest records;
- laboratory reporting packages;
- non-public standards candidates and approval notes.

## Access posture

The private analytical workbench is not a general public site feature. It is a separate, access-controlled workflow. Public reference pages may explain how the workbench is intended to be used, but they do not expose the confidential datasets or decisions handled there.

## Where to send the work

If the question depends on confidential inputs rather than published Heavy Metal Index pages, do not use the public Ask the Index assistant. Use the private workbench workflow or the contact routes described on [contact](/contact).
